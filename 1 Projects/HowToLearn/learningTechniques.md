				Pomodoro Technique

		ALGORITHM 

1.) Work
	Set a timer for 25 minutes and do focused work without distractions during this time (each 25-minute work/study
	block is called a 'pomodoro')

2.) Rest
	Take a 5-minute break 

3.) Repeat
	Complete 4 consecutive work/study sessions and then take a 20 to 30 min break


		BENEFITS

1.) Boost Focus:
	Multitasking is usually a source of distraction. The short strict work sessions usually prevent this

2.) Reduce Procrastination:
	Procrastination is often induced by start paralysis usually due to huge work load/bulk to be realised
	Breaking the work into short 25-minute chunks tricks the mind to not see the whole work load at once

3.) Prevents Burnout:
	Frequent and structured breaks forces the mind to rest and refresh increasing capacity for work without fatigue




				Flash Cards and Spaced Repetition


1.) Rational:
	I am not so keen on memorization. I love to learn how to arrive at something rather than just memorizing. I can
	memorize when necessary but I don't like it very much...hence I was more into Physics and Maths than Biology!
	
	Nevertheless, there are just some topics and stuff that just need to be memorized and the best way to do that
	so that it actually sticks and can be recalled when needed is to deeply memorize by repetition. Here is where
	the power this technique shines through!

2.) Algorithm:
	Create Flash cards of concepts and terms that are to be learned

	Initially,several times during the day, test (quiz) yourself and see if you know the content of the flashcards

	Reduce the testing/quizzing frequency as the material is better memorized (progress is gauged by how well you score
	when comparing your answers to the flash cards)

3.) Applicability:
	Especially suitable for things like certifications
	A helpful program for this is Anki, but I may have to experiment to find apps or other systems better suited for me
	Incorporate the flash cards in a note taking system (like obsidian) in order to have them at hand in case you need
	to recall something
