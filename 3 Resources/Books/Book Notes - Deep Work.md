# Book Notes: Deep Work

**Author:** Cal Newport
**Status:** Reading

## Core Idea

Deep work = professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that push your cognitive capabilities to their limit. This is the kind of work that creates value and is hard to replicate.

## Key Takeaways

- Shallow work (emails, Slack, meetings) feels productive but rarely moves the needle
- The ability to do deep work is becoming increasingly rare and increasingly valuable
- Schedule deep work blocks — don't wait for inspiration
- "Attention residue" is real: every time you switch tasks, part of your brain is still on the previous task

## How I Want to Apply This

- Block 2 hours every morning for deep work (no Slack, no email)
- Use a "shutdown ritual" at end of workday to mentally close open loops
- Track deep work hours per week — aim for 15+ hours
- Batch shallow work into specific time slots (11am, 3pm)

## Favorite Quotes

- The idea that you should train your focus like you train a muscle
- If you don't produce, you won't thrive — no matter how talented you are
