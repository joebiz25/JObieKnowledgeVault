# Book Notes: Digital Minimalism

**Author:** Cal Newport
**Rating:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

## Core Idea

A philosophy of technology use where you focus your time on a small number of carefully selected activities that strongly support things you value, and then happily miss out on everything else.

## Key Takeaways

- Most people's relationship with their phone is not intentional — it's the result of careful engineering by apps designed to capture attention
- Solitude is essential for clear thinking. Many people haven't experienced true solitude (no input from other minds) in years
- Don't start with "what should I remove?" — start with "what do I value?" and only keep tech that serves those values
- The "digital declutter": 30 days without optional technology, then reintroduce only what passes a strict test

## What I Changed After Reading This

- Removed social media apps from my phone (only use on desktop, intentionally)
- Turned off all notifications except calls and messages from family
- Started taking walks without headphones — just thinking
- Phone stays out of the bedroom
